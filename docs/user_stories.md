# User Stories

## User Story 1: Displaced Single Worker Maintaining Employment

**As a** displaced renter who is still employed,
**I want** to understand my current recovery stage and receive prioritized guidance on housing and financial stability,
**So that** I can continue working without falling into financial crisis.

### Acceptance Criteria

- User can input or infer displacement status after evacuation
- System identifies recovery stage as post-evacuation displacement
- User receives:
  - local temporary housing options
  - renter-specific aid eligibility guidance
  - expected insurance and assistance timelines
- Guidance is clear, sequenced, and non-overwhelming

## User Story 2: Family Managing Job Loss, School Disruption, and Dual Work Pressures

**As a** parent in a displaced household where one income has been lost and children’s schooling is disrupted,
**I want** guidance tailored to my family’s recovery stage that addresses income replacement and school continuity,
**So that** my family can maintain stability while navigating multiple disruptions at once.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Household is classified into a financial strain onset recovery stage
- System identifies:
  - income disruption
  - presence of school-age children
- User receives:
  - job search and retraining resources
  - school transfer and online learning options
  - a decision checklist prioritized by urgency
- Guidance acknowledges time pressure and emotional load

## User Story 3: Worker with Health Constraints Preventing Return Home

**As a** displaced worker with a health condition that makes returning home unsafe,
**I want** recovery guidance that accounts for long-term or permanent relocation needs,
**So that** I can maintain my health while staying employed in the region.

### Acceptance Criteria

- User can indicate or infer health-related displacement
- System categorizes recovery stage as non-returnable displacement
- User receives:
  - health-informed housing and relocation guidance
  - information on workplace accommodations
  - clarity on long-term insurance and aid implications
- Guidance avoids suggesting unsafe return options

## User Story 4: Worker Dependent on a Small Business with Uncertain Reopening

**As a** worker whose employer may not reopen after a wildfire,
**I want** insight into likely business recovery timelines and income alternatives,
**So that** I can make informed decisions about waiting, transitioning, or retraining.

### Acceptance Criteria

- System identifies employment tied to a wildfire-impacted business
- User is classified into a workforce instability recovery stage
- User receives:
  - estimated reopening likelihood or uncertainty signals
  - unemployment and disaster aid guidance
  - temporary work and retraining resources
- Guidance clearly communicates uncertainty and assumptions

## User Story 5: Caregiver Balancing Displacement, Work, and Elder Care

**As a** caregiver managing displacement while supporting an elderly dependent and maintaining employment,
**I want** coordinated recovery guidance that accounts for caregiving and accessibility needs,
**So that** I can avoid burnout while keeping my household stable.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Household is flagged as care-constrained recovery
- System recognizes:
  - caregiving responsibilities
  - healthcare continuity needs
- User receives:
  - healthcare access and provider transition guidance
  - housing options that consider accessibility
  - workplace flexibility and accommodation resources
- Guidance is structured to reduce cognitive overload
